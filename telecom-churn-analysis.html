The challenge

Telecom operators lose customers every month, but without breaking churn down by contract type, tenure, and payment method, it's hard to know where to focus retention efforts first.

The approach

  • Queried and segmented the customer base in SQL by contract type, tenure, and payment method.
  • Calculated churn rate for each segment to find where cancellations concentrated.
  • Built a cohort retention analysis to see how customer retention evolves month over month.
  • Delivered the findings as a Power BI report plus a one-page executive summary in French, matching the format used internally at Moroccan telecoms.

The result

Customers on month-to-month contracts churned at a substantially higher rate than those on annual contracts — the kind of finding that turns a vague 'reduce churn' goal into a specific retention target.

  • Churn broken down by 3 segment types
  • Cohort retention view built from raw transaction history
  • 1-page executive summary delivered in French

What I'd do differently

The SQL segmentation was the easy part — the harder part was deciding which cuts of the data were actually useful to a retention team versus just interesting to me. I'd involve a stakeholder earlier next time to prioritize which segments to dig into first.
