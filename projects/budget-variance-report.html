The challenge

Finance teams need to know where spending is drifting from budget, by department and by month, without waiting for a manual reconciliation at quarter-end.

The approach

  • Built the core budget-vs-actuals model in Excel first, matching the format finance stakeholders already work in.
  • Structured the data by department, month, revenue, and opex.
  • Rebuilt the reporting layer in Power BI with DAX measures for variance %.
  • Added RAG (red/amber/green) status indicators and a waterfall chart to show how the numbers move month to month.

The result

The report turns a spreadsheet reconciliation exercise into a live view: any department drifting from budget shows up immediately with a RAG flag instead of waiting to be found during the next manual review.

  • Budget vs actuals tracked by department and month
  • RAG status flags variance automatically
  • Waterfall chart shows month-over-month movement

What I'd do differently

Starting in Excel before Power BI was the right call — it forced me to get the business logic right with stakeholders who think in spreadsheets, before locking anything into DAX. I'd keep that order for any finance-facing report.
